# Phase 4 — Controlled Irregularity

## Purpose

Break excessive perfection without losing art direction. This phase should make the field feel more organic while keeping the overall system calm, coherent, and intentional.

## Dependencies

- Phases 1 through 3 are approved
- The team agrees that any irregularity must be integrated into the field itself
- No decorative post layer is added here

## Objective

Introduce a restrained amount of irregularity that:
- reduces mechanical perfection
- preserves pressure-like blending
- remains noticeable only in aggregate

## Scope

- Add subtle irregularity to the field itself
- Keep it integrated into the swell behavior rather than as a decorative overlay

## Phase deliverables

- Controlled irregularity layer
- Approved noise / warp parameter ranges

## Exit criteria

- Ripples feel less mechanically perfect
- Shapes remain coherent and calm
- Irregularity is visible only in aggregate, not as obvious wobble

## Stage breakdown

### Stage 4.1 — Spatial warp tests

**Goal**
Introduce barely perceptible spatial variation.

**Implementation tasks**
- Test slight coordinate warp
- Test restrained contour drift
- Test very small directional-bias variation
- Keep all effects bounded and reversible

**Checks**
- The field looks less synthetic
- Warp does not create visible flutter or wobble
- Overlap still reads as pooled pressure

### Stage 4.2 — Thickness variation tests

**Goal**
Make strong swells feel less uniform without changing their speed.

**Implementation tasks**
- Add local width modulation
- Explore per-source asymmetry controls
- Link breadth variation to strength where helpful
- Keep the overall field profile soft and stable

**Checks**
- Stronger impacts feel richer, not noisier
- Width variation remains subtle and art-directed
- The core soft-swell identity is preserved

### Stage 4.3 — Variation strategy

**Goal**
Define how randomness enters the system without taking over.

**Implementation tasks**
- Set per-source randomization rules
- Set per-grid subtle variation rules
- Define seeds, bounds, and repeatability requirements
- Decide what is deterministic for debugging and review

**Checks**
- Variation is tunable rather than accidental
- Multiple sessions still belong to the same aesthetic world
- Debugging and side-by-side comparisons remain practical

### Stage 4.4 — Overlap validation

**Goal**
Ensure irregularity does not degrade the strongest part of the effect.

**Implementation tasks**
- Re-test overlap under repeated clicks and typing
- Check for dirty intersections and unwanted hotspots
- Confirm that the field still feels calm at higher interaction density

**Checks**
- Irregularity improves realism without degrading clarity
- Repeated input remains premium-looking
- The field still feels controlled, not chaotic

## Hand-off to next phase

At the end of Phase 4, the field should feel organic enough for production composition. Phase 5 should focus on integrating the field with real content and product layouts.
